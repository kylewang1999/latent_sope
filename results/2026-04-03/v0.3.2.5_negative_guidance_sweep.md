# v0.3.2.5: Negative Guidance Sweep — Behavior Policy Subtraction Hurts

**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Notebook:** `experiments/2026-04-03/MVP_v0.3.2.5_negative_guidance_sweep.ipynb`
**SLURM job:** 7746783 (44 min on V100)
**Builds on:** v0.2.5.14d (positive-only guidance, rho=0.886 at alpha=0.4)

## Goal

Test SOPE-style negative guidance: subtract a behavior policy's score gradient to "de-bias" toward the target policy. Sweep negative guidance ratio lambda in [0.0, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 1.0] with positive guidance alpha fixed at 0.3.

Formula: `guide = target_grad - lam * behavior_grad`, applied to action dims only.

## Setup

- **Behavior policy:** BCGaussian (2-layer MLP, 256 hidden) trained on same 200 demos + 50 rollouts as chunk diffuser
- **Target policies:** 6 Lift diffusion policies (oracle SR: 8%–90%)
- **Diffuser:** Same as v0.2.5 series (chunk_size=4, 256 diffusion steps, base_dim=32, dim_mults=(1,4,8), action_weight=5)
- **Scorer:** RobomimicDiffusionScorer with score_timestep=5
- **Generation:** 50 synthetic trajectories per policy, T_GEN=60, gamma=1.0
- **Reward:** hard (cube_z > 0.84) and sigmoid (sharpness=150)
- 6 lambda values x 6 policies = 36 guided runs + 1 unguided baseline

### Notable observations at high lambda

- **lam=0.7:** Ranking collapses. 50demos_epoch30 reaches 90% SR (above its oracle 82%), while 200demos_epoch40 drops to 40%. State logRMSE degrades to -1.78 (trajectories become unrealistic).
- **lam=1.0:** Near-total collapse. All policies at 0–4% SR, hard returns near zero. Sigmoid still shows some ordering (rho=0.714) but values are compressed.

## Analysis

**Negative guidance does not help.** The best configuration is lambda=0.0 (no behavior subtraction), which is identical to the positive-only guidance from v0.2.5.14d at alpha=0.3. Key findings:

1. **Monotonic degradation with lambda:** rho_hard drops from 0.829 → 0.372 as lambda increases. State logRMSE also degrades from -2.27 → -1.64, indicating trajectories become less realistic.

2. **Behavior subtraction suppresses success:** Even small lambda=0.1 drops overall SR substantially. The behavior policy gradient pulls trajectories away from successful regions.

3. **Non-uniform suppression across policies:** The negative guidance doesn't uniformly de-bias — it seems to disproportionately suppress weaker policies (whose actions are closer to the behavior policy), destroying rank ordering.

4. **Alpha=0.3 vs 0.4:** At lambda=0.0, rho_hard=0.829 here vs 0.886 in v0.2.5.14d at alpha=0.4. This is expected — alpha=0.3 was chosen to leave room for negative guidance, but it turns out the optimal strategy is pure positive guidance at alpha=0.4.
