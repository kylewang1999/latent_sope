**Date:** 2026-04-03

**SLURM Job:** 7748073 (d11-04, 2h05m)
**Builds on:** v0.3.2.5 (negative guidance sweep, best at lam=0) + v0.2.5.14d (alpha=0.4, rho=0.886)

## Goal

Joint sweep of positive guidance strength (alpha) and negative guidance strength (lam)
using a 15-point Maximin Latin Hypercube design over alpha in [0.1, 0.5], lam/alpha in [0, 1].
Formula: `guide = alpha * target_grad - lam * behavior_grad`

Behavior policy: BCGaussian trained on 200 demos + 50 rollouts.

## Setup

- 6 target policies: 10demos_e10 (8%), 100demos_e20 (42%), test (54%), 10demos_e30 (62%), 50demos_e30 (82%), 200demos_e40 (90%)
- 15 LHS design points in (alpha, ratio) space, lam = alpha * ratio
- 50 synthetic trajectories per policy, T=60, chunk_size=4, 256 diffusion steps
- Normalized gradients, action-only guidance
- Unguided baseline: SR=60%, hard=5.020, sigmoid=7.992, state_logRMSE=-2.477

## Key Findings

1. **Negative guidance helps ranking.** The no-negative baseline (#1, ratio=0) only achieves rho=+0.371. Both best points (#2, #7) use ratio 0.53–0.65, confirming behavior subtraction improves policy discrimination.

2. **Sweet spot: low alpha + moderate ratio.** The two best configs are (alpha=0.11, ratio=0.65) and (alpha=0.26, ratio=0.53). Both have moderate negative guidance relative to positive. The effective lam values (0.072 and 0.138) are small in absolute terms.

3. **High alpha + high ratio destroys ranking.** #13 (alpha=0.42, ratio=0.97, lam=0.407) collapses to rho=+0.131. Aggressive guidance in both directions overwhelms the diffusion prior.

4. **logRMSE_H explodes at high guidance.** Points #13 and #14 have logRMSE_H > 10, indicating the OPE hard estimates are wildly off in magnitude even when sigmoid ranking (rho_sigm=+0.771) is decent. Sigmoid reward is more robust to trajectory degradation.

5. **State realism degrades with guidance.** stateLogRMSE goes from -2.45 (unguided) to -1.6 at aggressive settings. The best-ranking points (#2, #7) sit at -2.18 to -2.20 — moderate degradation.

6. **Regret@1 = 0.08 at best points.** Both #2 and #7 correctly identify the top policy (200demos_e40, 90%) as best, with only 0.08 regret. Low-ratio points (#1, #3, #9, #11) have R@1=0.280.

## Comparison to Prior Experiments

- **v0.3.2.5 (negative guidance sweep, 6 policies):** Best at lam=0 (no negative), rho=+0.829. That sweep used fixed alpha and swept lam independently, so lam>0 always hurt. Here, jointly tuning alpha+lam finds configs where negative guidance helps.
- **v0.2.5.14d (alpha=0.4, 6 policies):** rho=+0.886 with positive-only guidance. The best points here (+0.829) don't beat that, but the comparison isn't direct — different normalization and baseline diffuser.
