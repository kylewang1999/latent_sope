# v0.3.2.5c: Alpha x Lambda LHS Sweep (12 Policies)

**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Notebook:** `experiments/2026-04-03/MVP_v0.3.2.5c_12pol_lhs_sweep.ipynb`
**SLURM Job:** 7749261 (d14-18, ~3.5h)
**Builds on:** v0.3.2.5b (6-policy LHS sweep, best rho=+0.829) + v0.2.5.14e (12-policy action sweep)

## Goal

Same 15-point Maximin LHS design as v0.3.2.5b (alpha in [0.1, 0.5], ratio=lam/alpha in [0, 1]), but evaluated on all 12 target policies instead of 6. Tests whether the (alpha, ratio) sweet spot from v0.3.2.5b holds on the harder 12-policy ranking problem.

Formula: `guide = alpha * target_grad - lam * behavior_grad`

Behavior policy: BCGaussian trained on 200 demos + 50 rollouts.

## Setup

- 12 target policies spanning 0–90% oracle SR:
  50demos_e10 (0%), 10demos_e10 (8%), 200demos_e10 (18%), 200demos_e20 (24%), 100demos_e20 (42%), test (54%), 50demos_e20 (60%), 10demos_e30 (62%), 100demos_e30 (72%), 50demos_e30 (82%), 50demos_e40 (88%), 200demos_e40 (90%)
- 15 LHS design points in (alpha, ratio) space, lam = alpha * ratio
- 50 synthetic trajectories per policy per design point, T=60, chunk_size=4, 256 diffusion steps
- Normalized gradients, action-only guidance
- 180 guided runs total (12 policies x 15 design points)

## Key Findings

1. **Rank correlations drop sharply with 12 policies.** The best rho_hard=+0.552 (12 policies) is far below the v0.3.2.5b best of +0.829 (6 policies). With 12 policies including many closely-spaced oracle SRs (e.g., 42% vs 54% vs 60% vs 62%), the ranking problem is much harder.

2. **Sweet spot shifts to low alpha + low ratio.** The 6-policy best was at (alpha=0.11, ratio=0.65) — here that same config drops to rho=+0.284. The 12-policy best is at (alpha=0.18, ratio=0.30), suggesting moderate-strength positive guidance with less negative guidance works better when policies are harder to discriminate.

3. **Low-ratio points dominate.** The top-4 by rho_hard are all ratio <= 0.30: #4 (0.30), #3 (0.16), #11 (0.11), #9 (0.23). This contrasts with v0.3.2.5b where ratio=0.53–0.65 was optimal. With more policies, negative guidance hurts more than it helps — it may be adding noise that confuses nearby-policy discrimination.

4. **High ratio still catastrophic.** Points #13 (ratio=0.97) and #14 (ratio=0.82) collapse to rho near 0 or negative, with logRMSE_H > 9. Same failure mode as v0.3.2.5b.

5. **No-negative baseline (#1, ratio=0) is decent but not best.** rho_hard=+0.343 — worse than the best guided point (+0.552) but better than most high-ratio points. Small negative guidance can help, but the margin is slim.

6. **Sigmoid vs hard reward diverge at high ratio.** At extreme guidance (#13, #14), rho_sigm goes negative while rho_hard stays near 0. Sigmoid reward is no longer reliably more robust in the 12-policy setting.

7. **State realism correlates with ranking.** stateLogRMSE for the best points (#3, #4, #9, #11) ranges from -2.25 to -2.36, close to the unguided level. High-ratio points degrade to -1.6.

## Analysis

The core challenge with 12 policies is discriminating between closely-spaced policies (e.g., 100demos_e20 at 42% vs test at 54% vs 50demos_e20 at 60% vs 10demos_e30 at 62%). Negative guidance helps separate very distinct policies (like 8% vs 90%) but adds noise that hurts fine-grained discrimination. The optimal strategy shifts toward relying more on the target policy gradient alone (lower ratio) at slightly higher strength (alpha 0.15–0.18).

rho=+0.552 is still a meaningful positive correlation but far from the +0.886 achieved with 6 policies in v0.2.5.14d. Improving 12-policy ranking likely requires better trajectory quality (more training data, longer diffusion training) rather than further guidance tuning.
