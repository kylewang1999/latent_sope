## Goal

Sweep `score_timestep` in [1, 2, 5, 10, 20, 50] with end-to-end OPE evaluation on 6 policies. Tests whether the t=5-10 sweet spot found in v0.2.5.4's isolated gradient quality experiment holds when guidance is applied in the full stitching loop.

## Setup

- **6 target policies** (oracle SR 8%--90%)
- **Diffuser:** v0.2.5.2 EMA (200 demos + 50 rollouts, chunk_size=4, 256 steps)
- **Guidance:** positive-only, action_scale=0.01, normalize_grad=True
- **Sweep:** score_timestep in [1, 2, 5, 10, 20, 50]
- **50 synthetic trajectories** per policy, T=60, gamma=1.0
- **Total:** 36 guided + 1 unguided runs, ~41.6 min

## Key Findings

**Result: PARTIAL SUCCESS.** Best rho_sigmoid=0.886 at t=20 matches the best result from v0.2.5.14d (action_scale=0.4). Hard reward peaks at rho=0.771 (t=50).

1. **The isolated gradient sweet spot (t=5-10) does NOT hold end-to-end.** OPE ranking peaks at t=20-50, not t=5-10. Gradient quality and OPE quality are not the same thing — weaker but more stable gradients at higher timesteps produce better policy differentiation.

2. **Low timesteps (t=1-2) are terrible** (rho 0.2--0.4). Sharp gradients at low noise levels are too noisy or destabilizing in the stitching loop.

3. **RMSE decreases monotonically with timestep** (0.569 -> 0.557), meaning higher timesteps also improve absolute OPE calibration.

4. **Best regret performance:**
   - R@1=0.000 at t=5 and t=20 (correctly identifies best policy)
   - R@3=0.000 at t=20 (sigmoid) and t=50 (hard) — perfect top-3 identification

5. **Guided SR values compressed** into 40-72% range regardless of oracle SR (8-90%), indicating guidance shifts trajectories but differentiation comes from ranking, not absolute SR.

## Comparison — Score Timestep vs Action Scale (6-policy)

| Method | Best rho_hard | Best rho_sigmoid | Best setting |
|--------|---------------|------------------|-------------|
| v0.2.5.14 baseline | 0.714 | 0.714 | scale=0.01, t=5 |
| v0.2.5.14d (scale sweep) | 0.886 | 0.943 | scale=0.4, t=5 |
| **v0.2.5.14f (timestep sweep)** | **0.771** | **0.886** | **scale=0.01, t=20** |

Increasing score_timestep from 5 to 20 (at scale=0.01) achieves rho_sigmoid=0.886, matching what scale=0.4 achieved. Both approaches independently break the 0.71 ceiling. **Combining both (scale=0.4 + t=20) is the obvious next experiment.**
