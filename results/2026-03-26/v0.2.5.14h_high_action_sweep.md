# v0.2.5.14h: High Action Scale Sweep (0.5–1.5) — Confirms Peak at 0.3–0.4

**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Notebook:** `experiments/2026-03-26/MVP_v0.2.5.14h_high_action_sweep.ipynb`
**SLURM job:** 7610676 (49 min on V100)
**Builds on:** v0.2.5.14d (sweep 0.1–0.5, rho=0.886 at scale=0.4)

## Goal

Sweep action_scale in [0.5, 0.6, 0.7, 0.8, 0.9, 1.0, 1.5] to check whether the convex rho trend from v0.2.5.14d continues at higher scales or peaks and decays.

## Setup

- 7 action_scale values x 6 policies = 42 guided runs
- Same 6 policies as v0.2.5.14d (oracle SR 8%–90%)
- 50 synthetic trajectories per run, T_GEN=60, gamma=1.0
- Diffusion: 256 steps, chunk_size=4, action_weight=5.0, score_timestep=5

**Result: Confirms optimal scale is 0.3–0.4.** Higher scales degrade all metrics.

## Analysis

### Key findings

1. **Rho degrades sharply above 0.5.** rho_hard drops from +0.600 (scale=0.5) to +0.131 (scale=0.9) to NaN (scale>=1.0). The peak at scale=0.4 from v0.2.5.14d is confirmed as the optimum.

2. **SR collapses at high scales.** At scale>=1.0, ALL policies produce 0% hard SR — guidance is so strong it destroys trajectory quality, making rho_hard undefined (NaN due to constant returns).

3. **Sigmoid reward is more robust but noisy.** rho_sigmoid peaks at +0.829 at scale=0.8, but this is misleading — absolute sigmoid returns are very low (3.1–3.9, near the floor). The signal is mostly noise when hard SR is 0%.

4. **RMSE and logRMSE worsen monotonically** with scale, confirming trajectory fidelity degrades at these scales.

### Combined picture across all action sweep experiments

| Version | Scales | Best rho_hard | Best rho_sigmoid |
|---------|--------|---------------|------------------|
| v0.2.5.14c | 0.005–0.05 | +0.714 (0.01/0.05) | +0.771 (0.05) |
| v0.2.5.14d | 0.1–0.5 | **+0.886 (0.4)** | **+0.943 (0.3)** |
| **v0.2.5.14h** | **0.5–1.5** | +0.600 (0.5) | +0.829 (0.8) |

The rho curve is clearly convex with a peak at **scale=0.3–0.4**. Below 0.1, guidance is too weak to differentiate policies. Above 0.5, guidance is too strong and collapses trajectory quality.

## Next steps
