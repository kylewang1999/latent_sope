**Builds on:** v0.2.5.14b (12 policies, rho=0.21 at scale=0.01), v0.2.5.14d (6 policies, rho=0.886 at scale=0.4)

## Goal

Test whether the larger action scales (0.03--0.5) that broke the rho ceiling on 6 policies also fix the rho collapse seen in v0.2.5.14b's 12-policy evaluation.

## Setup

- **12 target policies** spanning oracle SR 0%--90%
- **Sweep:** action_scale in [0.03, 0.05, 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.5]
- **Diffuser:** TemporalUnet (dim=32, mults=(1,4,8)), 256 steps, chunk_size=4, action_weight=5.0
- **Scorer:** RobomimicDiffusionScorer, score_timestep=5, sigmoid_sharpness=150.0
- **50 synthetic trajectories** per policy, T=60, gamma=1.0
- **Total:** 72 guided + 1 unguided runs, ~83.4 min

## Analysis

**Result: PARTIAL FAILURE.** Larger action scales improve over v0.2.5.14b's rho=0.21, but the best 12-policy rho (0.622 sigmoid at scale=0.5) is far below the 6-policy result (0.886--0.943 at scales 0.3--0.4). The 12-policy problem is fundamentally harder:

1. **Monotonic improvement with scale:** rho increases steadily from 0.03 to 0.5, suggesting even larger scales might help further. But v0.2.5.14d showed scale=0.5 starts causing collapse for some policies.

2. **Mid-range policies are the problem:** Guidance dramatically boosts some policies (50demos_epoch20: 60% oracle -> 94% synthetic SR) but collapses others (test_checkpoint: 54% oracle -> 8% SR). This creates ranking noise.

3. **Sigmoid reward consistently outperforms hard reward** for ranking, confirming v0.2.5.14d's finding.

4. **The 6->12 policy rho gap** (0.886 -> 0.622) suggests the method differentiates good-from-bad but struggles with fine-grained ranking among similar-quality policies.

#### Key observations

1. **Same rho-reconstruction tradeoff as 14d.** State logRMSE degrades from -2.427 (scale=0.03) to -1.872 (scale=0.5). The best ranking (rho=0.622 at scale=0.5) comes at the cost of ~0.6 worse logRMSE vs unguided (-2.477).

2. **Scale=0.5 causes uniform state degradation.** At scale=0.5, all 12 policies cluster in a narrow logRMSE band (-1.72 to -1.96), regardless of oracle SR. The guidance overwhelms policy-specific dynamics.

3. **Weaker policies deviate more at moderate scales** (consistent with 14d). At scale=0.3, 50demos_e10 (0%) has logRMSE=-2.201 while 200demos_e40 (90%) has -2.458. But at scale=0.5 this distinction collapses.

4. **The tradeoff is steeper for 12 policies than 6.** In 14d, scale=0.4 achieved rho=0.886 with logRMSE=-2.022. Here, scale=0.5 only reaches rho=0.622 with logRMSE=-1.872 — worse ranking *and* worse reconstruction, suggesting the guidance struggles more with fine-grained policy distinctions.
