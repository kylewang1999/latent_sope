## Analysis

### What improved
- **10x action_scale widened OPE spread** — guided hard range [2.96, 4.40] vs v0.2.5.13's [3.74, 4.58]. The guidance now has enough strength to visibly steer trajectories differently per policy.
- **Rank ordering mostly correct** — the lowest oracle policy (8%) gets the lowest guided OPE (2.96), and the highest oracle policy (90%) gets the highest guided OPE (4.40).
- **rho improved from +0.49 to +0.71** — a substantial jump from just increasing action_scale.

### What's holding rho back
- **test_checkpoint (54%) is an outlier** — its guided OPE (3.34) is unexpectedly low, ranking between the 8% and 42% policies instead of in the middle. Without this outlier, the rank ordering would be nearly perfect.
- **50demos_epoch30 (82%) also slightly out of order** — guided OPE 3.86 is lower than the 42% and 62% policies.

### Comparison across versions

| Version | action_scale | Spearman rho | Guided OPE range |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| v0.2.5.6 | 0.001 | -0.2571 | 58%-62% (SR) |
| v0.2.5.13 | 0.001 | +0.4857 | 7.15-7.74 (sigmoid) |
| **v0.2.5.14** | **0.01** | **+0.7143** | **6.24-7.46 (sigmoid)** |

The trend is clear: stronger guidance improves rank correlation. Further scaling (e.g., 0.02-0.05) may push rho above 0.8.
