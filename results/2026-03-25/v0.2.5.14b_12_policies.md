# v0.2.5.14b: 12-Policy Evaluation — Rho Collapse

**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Notebook:** `experiments/2026-03-25/MVP_v0.2.5.14b_12_policies.ipynb`

## Goal

Expand from 6 to 12 target policies (oracle SR spanning 0%-90%) using the same setup as v0.2.5.14. Test whether rho=0.71 holds at larger scale.

## Setup

- **Diffuser:** v0.2.5.2 EMA model (200 demos + 50 rollouts, chunk_size=4, 256 steps)
- **Guidance:** action_scale=0.01, score_timestep=5, positive-only, normalize_grad=True, sigmoid_sharpness=150
- **Generation:** 50 synthetic trajectories per policy, T_GEN=60, gamma=1.0
- **Scorer:** RobomimicDiffusionScorer (diffusion policy log-prob)

**Result: FAILURE** — rho collapsed from 0.71 (6 policies) to ~0.21 (12 policies), not statistically significant.

## Analysis

### Why rho collapsed

1. **Low-SR policies get inflated OPE.** The 0% and 18% oracle policies receive guided SR of 50-74% and guided hard OPE of 3.06-4.82. The guidance cannot distinguish weak policies from strong ones — 200demos_epoch10 (18% oracle) actually gets the *highest* guided hard OPE (4.82) of all 12 policies.

2. **OPE estimates are nearly flat.** Guided hard ranges from 2.96 to 4.82 across a 0%-90% oracle spread. The guidance signal at action_scale=0.01 is too weak to create meaningful separation at this scale.

3. **Per-policy MSE grows monotonically with oracle SR.** Low-SR policies have near-zero MSE (overestimated), high-SR policies have large MSE ~0.6 (underestimated). The method systematically fails at the extremes.

4. **Regret@1 = 0.72** — the OPE-top-ranked policy (200demos_epoch10, 18% oracle) is far from the true best (200demos_epoch40, 90% oracle).

### Comparison to v0.2.5.14 (6 policies)

The 6 policies from v0.2.5.14 are a subset of these 12. The rank ordering among those 6 was decent (rho=0.71), but adding 6 new policies — especially the low-SR epoch10 checkpoints — broke the ranking because guidance inflates their OPE estimates.

### Implication

action_scale=0.01 is insufficient for 12-policy ranking. The action sweep (v0.2.5.14c) should determine whether stronger guidance can recover ranking quality.
