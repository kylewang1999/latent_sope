## Goal

Sweep action_scale across [0.005, 0.01, 0.02, 0.03, 0.05] on 6 target policies to find the optimal scale for Spearman rho.

## Setup

- Same diffuser, scorer, and guidance config as v0.2.5.14
- 5 action_scale values x 6 policies = 30 guided runs
- 50 synthetic trajectories per run, T_GEN=60, gamma=1.0

## Analysis

### Non-monotonic relationship

Rho does not increase monotonically with action_scale. It peaks at 0.01, *drops* at 0.02-0.03, then recovers at 0.05. Intermediate scales disrupt trajectory quality without providing enough signal for ranking.

### Scale 0.01 vs 0.05 tradeoff

- **0.01:** Lower RMSE (0.565 vs 0.585), better R@1 (0.000 vs 0.280). More conservative guidance that preserves trajectory quality and correctly picks the top policy.
- **0.05:** Better sigmoid rho (0.771 vs 0.714), better R@3 (0.000 vs 0.133). Stronger guidance gives better overall ranking and top-3 identification, but misidentifies the single best policy.

### RMSE increases monotonically with scale

Stronger guidance pushes trajectories further from reality, increasing absolute error even when ranking improves. This is the fundamental tension: stronger guidance = better ranking signal but worse calibration.

### Key per-policy observations

- At scale=0.05, the worst policy (10demos_epoch10, 8% oracle) drops sharply to 26% guided SR — good separation from strong policies.
- But the best policy (200demos_epoch40, 90% oracle) also drops to only 46% guided SR, showing strong guidance degrades good policies too.

## Implications

- action_scale=0.01 remains the best default for this 6-policy set (good rho + best R@1).
- No scale tested breaks the rho=0.71 ceiling. The bottleneck may not be guidance strength but the scorer quality, diffuser capacity, or the chunk-stitching approach itself.
- The v0.2.5.14b result (rho collapse to 0.21 at 12 policies with scale=0.01) suggests the method's ranking ability is fragile and scale-dependent.
